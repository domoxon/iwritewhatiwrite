Cool Sh*t I Found This Week

Does what it says on the tin. A good week on the internet.

01

21st.dev

21st.dev

As a novice to coding, throwing around the fact I vaguely know the function of css at multiple social gatherings, this site thrills me. It is as if the Jedi Knights wrote an Ikea level of detail book rightly named How To Jedi for Beginners. Having access to this array of right clickable code, and being able to amend through prompts is fantastic.

Nothing short of what my grandfather fought for, easy AI prompt website design. Vibe coding if you don't want to sound so black and white.

To the select few individuals I know I gave this website too before, I hear you sighing in grief. Now I intend to tell more people, and spread the good news. 21st.dev is not simply a game changer, but a game winner of a website.

02

Monkeytype

monkeytype.com

A very faint but apparent memory I have is being envious. Probably around the age of 6, I remember seeing my father touch type for the first time. It was one of the coolest things I'd ever seen at the time, and by then Pokemon Pearl had been in my life for quite some time, it was close to even that.

From that moment, I had never wanted to do something so badly. Of course, this is 2006-ish, the world had not quite been exploited by screens at this point. It was a thing of the future.

This website has put my 19 years of practice to test.

Think of it as digital gymnastics I suppose.

03

Earth Reviews

neal.fun/earth-reviews

I, for one, was mortified to discover that Earth only rates yawning a 3/5.

The morning yawn is a human right, or still, a living organism right. A glorious act. 3 stars out of 5 is not a compelling result.

Dogs deservingly got 4.5 stars, whilst laws are a correctly chosen 2 stars.

Fun little emotion jerking site developed by Neal, who has a portfolio to make your day, can find his work at neal.fun

04

Godly

godly.website

I don't think I've ever got lost on a site so quickly. If you're at all active on Reddit, you'll be aware of Godly and probably have been for quite some time.

Godly is an almighty tool for website inspo, I'd love to say I spend a lot of time on there, but to be frank, every site example is so perfectly placed, executed and displayed that in reality, I select one and spend quite some time admiring it.

Designs that draw you in. The best kind.

05

Pointer Pointer

pointerpointer.com

This I found in the very depths. So deep into the digital abyss in fact, that I've begun to debate as to whether the images are AI generated. I'd like to believe they aren't and this is just a true gem.

Absolutely pointless, but edge of your seat entertainment.

That's a wrap for Week 1. See you next week.
